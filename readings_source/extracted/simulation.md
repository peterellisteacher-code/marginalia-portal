How is ‘Reality’ Defined?
The metaphysical scenario of Simulation Theory refers to something which philosophers have discussed for centuries: how do we know if what we’re seeing is ‘real’?
Rizwan Virk writes: “the findings of quantum physics may shed some doubt on the fact that the material universe is real. The more that scientists look for the ‘material’ in the material world, the more they find that it doesn’t exist.”

How is ‘Reality’ Defined? Physicist David Bohm:
Reality is what is true.

We perceive what we pay attention to.
We pay attention to what we think about.

Objections
Paul Davies says “Mathematicians have proved that a universal computing machine can create an artificial world that is itself capable of simulating its own world, and so on ad infinitum.
In other words, simulations nest inside simulations inside simulations,”
“Because fake worlds can outnumber real ones without restriction, the ‘real’ multiverse would inevitably spawn a vastly greater number of virtual multiverses.”

[Slide 27]
Objection: Harvard University physicist Lisa Randall
“It’s just not based on well-defined probabilities,” Randall said.
“The argument says you’d have lots of things that want to simulate us. I actually have a problem with that.
We mostly are interested in ourselves. Why simulate us? I mean, there’s so many things to be simulating. … I don’t know why this higher species would want to bother with us.”
